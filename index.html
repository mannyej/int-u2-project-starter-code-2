Water scarcity is a pressing global issue affecting billions of people worldwide. This website explores the causes behind shrinking supplies, the social and economic impacts of limited access, and the practical solutions communities can adopt to build resilience and protect clean water for future generations.

What is Water Scarcity?

Water scarcity happens when demand for fresh water exceeds supply, or when water quality is too poor for use. Today, over 2 billion people live in countries facing high water stress.

Fresh water is limited, and many regions are already seeing shortages.

Causes and Impacts

  • Climate change increases droughts and uneven rainfall.
  • Pollution contaminates rivers, lakes, and groundwater.
  • Population growth and agriculture drive water demand higher.

Agriculture uses about 70% of the world’s freshwater supply.

Solutions and Actions

Small choices add up. Saving water and supporting clean water access can help communities thrive.

  • Fix leaks and use water-saving fixtures.
  • Support reuse and rainwater harvesting programs.
  • Protect wetlands and invest in water treatment.

Innovation and conservation are key to restoring water resilience.
